Why This Tour

Why Cruise Passengers Choose This Eco Adventure

This tour is a great fit for cruise passengers who want to experience a different side of St Thomas. Rather than focusing only on beaches or shopping, it gives guests the chance to explore protected waters, mangroves and marine life with local guidance.

The Mangrove Lagoon is one of the most ecologically significant areas in St Thomas, and paddling through it by kayak is a genuinely different kind of Caribbean experience that most cruise passengers don't get elsewhere on their itinerary.

Combined with a guided snorkeling session in protected waters, the result is a shore excursion that's active without being exhausting, educational without being dry, and memorable without being expensive.

Day at a Glance

What to Expect on the Day

A typical St Thomas Kayak & Snorkel Adventure: from equipment briefing to open water and back.

1

Meet and Equipment Briefing

After meeting for the excursion, guests are introduced to the equipment and given instructions before heading onto the water. Guides cover kayaking technique, safety and what to expect on the route.

2

Kayak Through the Mangrove Lagoon

The kayaking portion takes place in calm protected waters inside the Mangrove Lagoon Wildlife Sanctuary and Marine Preserve. It is a scenic and peaceful way to experience a side of St Thomas most cruise visitors never see.

3

Snorkeling Briefing and Entry

After paddling, guides introduce the snorkeling equipment and explain what to look for underwater. The snorkeling takes place in a protected area with good water clarity and marine life visibility.

4

Explore the Underwater Environment

Once in the water, guests explore at their own pace with guide support. The exact experience can vary depending on weather, water conditions and the operator's route, but the focus is on nature, activity and marine scenery.

5

Return and Head Back to Port

After snorkeling, guests return via kayak or transport and make their way back to the cruise pier in time for sail away. You'll return energised, sun warmed and with a genuinely different story to tell.

FAQs

St Thomas Kayak & Snorkel Adventure FAQ

Common questions from cruise passengers planning this eco shore excursion.

Is this tour suitable for cruise passengers?
Yes. This type of kayak and snorkel tour can work well for cruise passengers, but you should always check the duration, meeting point and transfer details against your ship schedule. Confirm logistics with your operator before booking to make sure everything fits within your port day.

Do I need kayaking experience?
No specialist kayaking experience is usually required. Guides will brief you on technique and safety before you get on the water. The kayaking takes place in calm, protected waters which makes it more accessible than open ocean paddling. However, guests should be comfortable following guide instructions and paddling for an extended period.

Do I need to be able to swim?
You should be comfortable in the water for the snorkeling portion of the tour. You don't need to be an expert swimmer, but basic water confidence is important. Guests who are anxious in open water or unable to swim should consider a lower activity excursion instead.

Is this harder than a beach excursion?
Yes. This is more active than a standard beach excursion because it includes both kayaking and snorkeling. That said, the kayaking takes place in calm protected waters, so it is accessible for most reasonably active adults and older children. If you want a genuinely relaxing, low effort day, a beach excursion is the better choice.

Where does the tour take place?
The tour focuses on the Mangrove Lagoon Wildlife Sanctuary and Marine Preserve, located on the south east shore of St Thomas. This protected area offers calm paddling conditions and a natural Caribbean environment quite different from the beaches and shopping areas near the cruise pier.

Is this good for families?
It can be a good option for families with older children who are comfortable with kayaking, swimming and active outdoor experiences. Very young children and those who are not comfortable in the water or with physical activity may find this excursion challenging. Always check minimum age and weight requirements with your operator before booking.

What should I wear?
Wear swimwear under light clothing for easy transitions. Water shoes or secure sandals are recommended over flip flops, as they stay on your feet while kayaking and make reef entry easier. Avoid loose or baggy clothing that might interfere with paddling.

What should I bring?
Bring a towel, reef safe sunscreen (applied before departure), sunglasses, a hat, a dry bag or waterproof phone pouch, a change of clothes and cash or card for extras or transfers. Water shoes or secure sandals are also recommended for this type of tour.

Day at a Glance

What to Expect on the Day

A typical St Thomas Kayak, Hike & Snorkel Tour: from briefing to open water and back.

1

Meet and Equipment Briefing

After meeting for the excursion, guests are introduced to the equipment and given safety instructions. Guides cover kayaking technique, route expectations and what to bring into the water before anything starts.

2

Kayak Through the Mangrove Lagoon

The kayaking portion takes place in calm protected waters inside the Mangrove Lagoon Wildlife Sanctuary and Marine Preserve. The route takes you through scenic mangrove scenery on a side of St Thomas most cruise visitors never see.

3

Hike Cas Cay

The hiking section takes guests through natural terrain and coastal scenery around Cas Cay. Expect viewpoints, wild landscape and a genuine sense of being somewhere off the beaten track. Paths can be uneven, so appropriate footwear is important.

4

Snorkel in Clear Caribbean Water

Guests then have the opportunity to snorkel in clear Caribbean water with guide support. Equipment is provided and instructions given before entry. Explore the marine environment at your own pace.

5

Return to the Marina and Head Back

After snorkeling, guests return to the meeting point and make their way back to the cruise pier in time for sail away. You'll return to the ship energised, sun warmed and with a genuinely different St Thomas story to tell.

Note: Exact routes and timings can vary depending on weather, sea conditions and the operator's schedule. Always confirm the specific itinerary with your operator at booking.

Is This Tour Suitable for You?

This is one of the more active St Thomas shore excursions available to cruise passengers. Guests should be comfortable kayaking, walking over natural terrain, entering the water for snorkeling and spending time outdoors in warm weather, all in the same morning.

⚠️ Consider Another Tour If:

  • You prefer a relaxing beach day
  • You do not want to paddle or kayak
  • You are uncomfortable swimming or snorkeling
  • You have mobility concerns that affect water or walking activities
  • You do not want to walk over uneven or natural terrain
  • You prefer air conditioned sightseeing

Prefer something more relaxed? St Thomas beach excursions offer a lower activity alternative with sun, sand and Caribbean water at your own pace.

FAQs

St Thomas Kayak, Hike & Snorkel Tour FAQ

Common questions from cruise passengers planning this eco shore excursion.

Is this tour suitable for cruise passengers?
Yes. This tour can work well for cruise passengers who want an active eco adventure, but always check the duration, meeting point and transfer details against your ship schedule. A taxi transfer to the marina may be required: confirm with your operator before booking.

How active is this tour?
This is an active excursion: more demanding than a beach day or standard sightseeing tour. It combines kayaking, hiking over natural terrain and snorkeling, all within a single morning. Guests should be physically comfortable with sustained outdoor activity in warm, humid conditions.

Do I need kayaking experience?
Specialist kayaking experience is not usually required. Guides will brief you on technique and safety before you get on the water, and the kayaking takes place in calm, protected waters. Guests should be comfortable paddling and following guide instructions for the duration of the kayak portion.

Do I need to be able to swim?
Yes. Guests should be comfortable in the water for the snorkeling portion of the tour. You don't need to be an expert swimmer, but basic water confidence is important. Guests who are anxious in open water or unable to swim should consider a lower activity excursion instead.

Where does the tour go?
The tour focuses on the Mangrove Lagoon Wildlife Sanctuary and Marine Preserve area, used for the kayaking portion, and Cas Cay, which features the hiking element. Both areas are on the south eastern side of St Thomas and offer a natural, less visited side of the island.

Is this good for children?
This tour can suit older children and teenagers who are comfortable with kayaking, walking on natural terrain and snorkeling. Very young children and those who are not confident in the water or with physical activity may find this excursion too demanding. Always check minimum age and weight requirements with your operator before booking.

What should I wear?
Wear swimwear under light clothing for easy transitions between activities. Water shoes or secure sandals are strongly recommended: flip flops are unsuitable for kayaking and particularly unsuitable for hiking on natural terrain. A hat and sun protection are essential throughout the day.

Is this better than the kayak and snorkel tour?
It depends on what you want. Choose this tour if you want the added hiking element and a more varied, demanding adventure that includes Cas Cay. Choose the kayak and snorkel tour if you want a slightly simpler, less physically intensive eco excursion without the hiking component.

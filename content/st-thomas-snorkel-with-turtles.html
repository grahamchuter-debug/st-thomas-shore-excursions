Day at a Glance

What to Expect on the Day

A typical St Thomas snorkel with turtles tour: from pier to open water and back.

1

Meet and Board

After meeting at the excursion departure point, guests board the vessel. The crew provides a welcome briefing and safety information while you prepare to set sail.

2

Sail to the Snorkeling Area

The boat sails towards the snorkeling site, often the Turtle Cove or Buck Island area. Enjoy the views and the Caribbean breeze while the crew prepares snorkeling equipment and shares guidance.

3

Snorkeling Briefing and Entry

The crew provides snorkeling instructions before guests enter the water. Equipment is available for use. Once in the water, you're free to explore the marine environment at your own pace.

4

Snorkel and Explore

Look for tropical fish, colourful coral, and marine life below the surface. If conditions are favourable, green sea turtles may be spotted in the area. Every session is different, and that's what makes it special.

5

Relax and Return

After snorkeling, there is usually time to relax on board, enjoy a drink, and take in the Caribbean views on the sail back. You'll return to the cruise pier well before sail away.

St Thomas Snorkel with Turtles FAQ

Answers to the most common questions from cruise passengers planning this excursion.

Can you snorkel with turtles in St Thomas?
Yes, some St Thomas snorkeling tours visit areas where guests may have the chance to see green sea turtles. Sightings are possible but never guaranteed, turtles are wild animals and their presence varies with conditions, season and behaviour.

Is this tour suitable for cruise passengers?
Yes. This style of tour is popular with cruise passengers, but always check the meeting location, tour duration and your ship's departure time before booking. Confirm these details with your operator to ensure a comfortable fit with your schedule.

Do I need to be a strong swimmer?
You don't need to be an expert swimmer, but you should be comfortable in the water and able to follow snorkeling instructions from the crew. This is a more active excursion than a beach day: guests who are anxious in open water may find a beach excursion a better fit.

How long is the tour?
This tour is usually around 3 hours and 30 minutes, making it a half day excursion that leaves time for other port activities. Always confirm the exact duration with your operator, as timings can vary slightly.

Where does the tour depart from?
Meeting details depend on your ship and docking location. Havensight pier is often walkable to departure points, while Crown Bay passengers may need a short taxi transfer. Confirm exact meeting instructions with your operator after booking.

Are turtle sightings guaranteed?
No. Turtles are wild animals and sightings cannot be guaranteed on any tour. The snorkeling areas visited are known for turtle activity, which gives guests a genuine chance to see them, but conditions, season and animal behaviour always play a role. The wider snorkeling experience is excellent regardless.

What should I bring on this tour?
Bring swimwear worn under your clothing, a towel, reef safe sunscreen, sunglasses, a hat, a waterproof phone pouch or dry bag, and cash or card for transfers, tips or extras. If you are prone to motion sickness, take tablets before departing.

Is this better than a beach day?
It depends entirely on what you want. Choose this snorkeling tour if you want a more active, memorable water experience with the chance to see turtles and tropical marine life. Choose a beach day if you prefer relaxing on sand with minimal physical activity. Both are excellent, it's a question of your style.

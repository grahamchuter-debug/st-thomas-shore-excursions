The Beach Guide

St Thomas Has Some of the
Caribbean's Finest Beaches

St Thomas is blessed with dozens of beautiful beaches, but not all of them are equally suited to cruise passengers with limited time ashore. The best beaches for cruise ship visitors balance accessibility, facilities, and that pure Caribbean wow factor, so you spend your day in the water, not in transit.

Whether you're after a wide sandy bay to relax on a sun lounger, a coral reef teeming with marine life, or a secluded stretch of shore away from the crowds, St Thomas has a beach for every style of traveller. Our St Thomas beach excursions make it easy to get to the best spots directly from the cruise port.

Beach #1 · Most Iconic

Magens Bay

Consistently rated among the most beautiful beaches in the world, Magens Bay is St Thomas's showpiece, a heart shaped, mile long arc of powdery white sand lapped by the calmest, clearest water you'll find on the island. For cruise passengers visiting St Thomas for the first time, Magens Bay is the quintessential Caribbean beach experience.

Why cruise passengers love it

  • ✓ The calm, protected water is ideal for swimming at any level, including children and non swimmers
  • ✓ Full facilities on the beach: sun loungers, umbrellas, snorkel rental, restrooms, snack bars and a beach bar
  • ✓ The sweeping mountain backdrop and turquoise bay create stunning photos at every turn
  • ✓ Reliably organised and accessible, a safe, easy choice for groups and families

📋 Things to know

  • • Small entrance fee (usually $5 per person, cash or card)
  • • ~35 minutes from Havensight, ~45 from Crown Bay by taxi
  • • Popular on busy port days, arrive early for the best spots
  • • No live coral reef, swimming and paddling rather than snorkeling

Beach #2 · Most Scenic

Sapphire Beach

Named for the extraordinary depth of colour of its water, Sapphire Beach lives up to its name in every way. The beach sits in a natural bay on the east side of the island, with calm, jewel bright water, a living coral reef just offshore, and views across to the neighbouring British Virgin Islands. It's a favourite for good reason: beautiful, accessible, and endlessly rewarding to explore.

Why cruise passengers love it

  • ✓ The coral reef just 50 yards from shore makes it one of the best snorkeling beaches in St Thomas
  • ✓ Crystal clear, calm water in a protected bay, perfect conditions for snorkeling and swimming
  • ✓ Beach bar, snorkel equipment rental, and sun loungers available on the beach
  • ✓ Stunning views across the Sir Francis Drake Channel to neighbouring islands

📋 Things to know

  • • ~25 minutes from Havensight, one of the most accessible good beaches
  • • Public beach, no entrance fee
  • • Snorkel gear available to rent on the beach ($10 to 15 typical)
  • • The reef is best explored at mid morning when light angles in beautifully

Beach #3 · Most Secluded

Secret Harbor

Tucked into a sheltered cove on St Thomas's south eastern shore, Secret Harbor is a gem that rewards those who venture a little off the well worn path. The beach is smaller and less visited than Magens Bay or Sapphire, which gives it a quieter, more intimate feel, ideal for couples or anyone who wants a peaceful morning by crystal clear water. The reef just offshore is home to a variety of marine life.

Why cruise passengers love it

  • ✓ Calmer and less crowded than the island's most popular beaches
  • ✓ Excellent snorkeling directly from the beach with colourful reef fish and sea grass
  • ✓ The area is a known sea turtle habitat. there's a real chance to see turtles while snorkeling
  • ✓ Resort beach bar on site for food, drinks and snorkel rentals

📋 Things to know

  • • ~30 minutes from Havensight by taxi
  • • Smaller beach, best suited to smaller groups
  • • A resort fronts the beach but it remains a public beach
  • • Turtle sightings are possible but can never be guaranteed. wildlife is wild!

Beach #4 · Most Peaceful

Lindquist Beach

Part of the Smith Bay Park, Lindquist Beach is widely considered one of the most beautiful beaches in St Thomas: yet it sees far fewer visitors than Magens Bay or Sapphire Beach, making it a wonderful choice for those wanting a more tranquil beach day. The sand is fine and powdery white, the water calm and clear, and the surrounding trees provide natural shade along the shoreline.

Why cruise passengers love it

  • ✓ One of the least crowded good beaches in St Thomas: feels like a private paradise
  • ✓ Beautiful white sand and calm, crystal clear turquoise water
  • ✓ Natural tree shade along the beach, ideal for those avoiding direct sun
  • ✓ Small entrance fee helps keep the numbers down and the beach pristine

📋 Things to know

  • • ~30 minutes from Havensight; small park entrance fee applies
  • • Fewer facilities than larger beaches, bring your own snacks and water
  • • Not always on the standard taxi beach run route, best reached on a tour
  • • Snorkeling is possible but the reef isn't as developed as Sapphire or Coki

Beach #5 · Best Snorkeling

Coki Beach

If snorkeling is your priority, Coki Beach is the answer. The reef starts immediately offshore and the underwater visibility is exceptional, often 30 feet or more. This compact beach sits next to Coral World Ocean Park, making it easy to combine a snorkeling session with a visit to one of the Caribbean's best marine wildlife attractions. The marine life here is some of the most prolific in St Thomas waters.

Why cruise passengers love it

  • ✓ The best snorkeling of any beach in St Thomas: reef starts right at the waterline
  • ✓ Abundant tropical fish: parrotfish, angelfish, sergeant majors, and more
  • ✓ Snorkel gear, beach chairs and food available to rent or buy on the beach
  • ✓ Coral World Ocean Park is a short walk away, perfect for a combined day out

📋 Things to know

  • • ~25 minutes from Havensight: accessible and quick from either port
  • • The beach is compact and fills up quickly, go early
  • • Vendors on the beach are friendly but persistent, set expectations early
  • • The water can be busy with snorkelers; watch your fins around other swimmers

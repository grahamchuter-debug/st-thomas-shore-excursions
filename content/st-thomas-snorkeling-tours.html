Why St Thomas Is a Brilliant
Snorkeling Port

St Thomas is one of the best Caribbean cruise ports for guests who want more than a simple beach day. With warm clear water, protected marine areas, mangrove lagoons and boat access snorkeling locations, the island offers several ways to experience the underwater side of the US Virgin Islands.

For cruise passengers, snorkeling tours are especially appealing because they combine adventure with structure. Instead of trying to arrange equipment, transport and timing independently, a guided snorkeling excursion can make the day easier, safer and more memorable.

Whether you want to sail towards Turtle Cove, kayak through the Mangrove Lagoon, or combine kayaking, hiking and snorkeling in one active shore excursion, St Thomas has options for different fitness levels and travel styles.

Before You Go

What To Expect on a
St Thomas Snorkeling Excursion

Most St Thomas snorkeling tours begin with either a short transfer or a meeting point near the cruise port, depending on the excursion and where your ship docks. Once checked in, guests are usually given instructions, safety guidance and equipment before heading out on the water.

Some tours focus on sailing and snorkeling from a boat, while others include kayaking through mangroves before entering the water. More active tours may also include hiking or walking over uneven terrain, so it is important to choose an excursion that suits your comfort level.

Expect warm weather, bright sun, clear water and a relaxed Caribbean atmosphere. Guests should be comfortable in the water and should always follow guide instructions.

Important Snorkeling Tour Tips

Before booking a snorkeling excursion, check the tour duration, meeting point, physical activity level and whether transport is included. Some tours are close to the cruise port; others require a taxi to a marina.

  • Check whether your ship docks at Havensight or Crown Bay, departure points vary
  • Confirm whether taxi transfers are included in the tour price
  • Choose a tour that matches your fitness and swimming ability
  • Book early during busy cruise weeks, small group tours fill quickly
  • Use reef safe sunscreen, it protects the marine environment
  • Listen carefully to guide instructions before entering the water

St Thomas Snorkeling Tours FAQ

Is St Thomas good for snorkeling?
Yes. St Thomas offers clear Caribbean water, diverse marine life, reef areas, turtle snorkeling opportunities and protected mangrove environments, making it one of the better cruise ports in the region for guests who want a water based excursion.

Can you snorkel with turtles in St Thomas?
Some St Thomas snorkeling tours visit areas where guests may have the chance to see green sea turtles, but as with all wildlife encounters, sightings can never be guaranteed. Tours visiting the Turtle Cove and Buck Island area offer some of the better opportunities for this experience.

Are St Thomas snorkeling tours suitable for cruise passengers?
Yes. Many snorkeling tours are designed for cruise passengers, but it is important to check the tour duration, departure point and return timing before booking. Some tours depart close to the cruise terminal, while others may require a short taxi transfer to a marina.

Do I need to be a strong swimmer?
You should be comfortable in the water for most snorkeling tours. Some excursions offer guidance or flotation support for less confident swimmers, but guests should choose a tour that honestly reflects their swimming ability. If you are not confident in open water, the sail and snorkel tour may have calmer conditions than the more active kayak and hiking options.

Are kayak and snorkel tours difficult?
Kayak and snorkel tours are more active than a standard beach excursion. Guests should be comfortable paddling and moving in and out of the water. The Mangrove Lagoon tours use calm, protected water which makes kayaking generally accessible, but guests should be honest about their fitness level before booking.

What is the most active snorkeling tour in St Thomas?
The kayak, hike and snorkel tour is typically one of the more physically demanding options because it combines paddling through the lagoon, walking over natural terrain at Cas Cay and snorkeling in open water. It is best suited to fit and active guests who want a more complete adventure than a standard beach or boat tour.

What should I wear for a snorkeling excursion?
Wear swimwear under light clothing and bring a towel, reef safe sunscreen, sunglasses and secure footwear such as water shoes or well fitting sandals. A change of clothes is useful for after the water. Bring a waterproof pouch for your phone and any valuables.

Should I book a snorkeling tour in advance?
Yes. Popular St Thomas snorkeling tours, particularly turtle snorkeling and small group eco kayak tours, can sell out during busy cruise periods. Booking ahead is recommended to secure your preferred tour and avoid disappointment on arrival.

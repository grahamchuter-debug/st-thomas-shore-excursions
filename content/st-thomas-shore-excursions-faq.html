St Thomas Cruise Port Basics

Where ships dock, how to get around the port area and whether you need an organised excursion.

Full Port Guide

Where do cruise ships dock in St Thomas?
Cruise ships usually dock at either Havensight (West Indian Company Dock) or Crown Bay. Both are in the Charlotte Amalie area, but they are separate cruise port locations with different proximity to shopping, transport and excursion pickups. Always check your itinerary before planning transport or excursion meeting points.

Is St Thomas easy to explore from a cruise ship?
Yes, St Thomas is a relatively straightforward and rewarding cruise port. Beaches, viewpoints, duty free shopping and a wide range of excursions are all within reach. That said, most of the best beaches and island tours require taxi transport or an organised excursion rather than walking directly from the terminal.

Can you walk from the St Thomas cruise port?
You can walk around parts of the port areas: particularly from Havensight, which has shopping at the pier and a walkable route into Charlotte Amalie. However, walking is not a practical option for beaches or island sightseeing. Crown Bay passengers typically rely more on taxis or organised excursions to reach attractions and beaches.

Which is better, Havensight or Crown Bay?
Neither is a bad option, but they suit different preferences. Havensight is often more convenient for nearby duty free shopping and has a shorter walk towards Charlotte Amalie. Crown Bay is well served by transport but may require a little more taxi planning for beaches and excursions. Both terminals have taxis available on exit.

Should I book a shore excursion in St Thomas?
It depends on what you want from your day. If you plan a simple beach visit, a taxi may be all you need. If you want multiple stops, snorkeling equipment, private transport or better timing control, a cruise friendly shore excursion typically makes the day easier, more enjoyable and less stressful to manage.

St Thomas Beach Excursion Questions

Which beaches to visit, how to get there and what to expect from a cruise day at the beach.

Beach Excursions Guide

What is the best beach in St Thomas for cruise passengers?
Magens Bay is the classic first time choice: calm, protected water and a sweeping sandy bay. Sapphire Beach is popular for its vivid blue water and light snorkeling opportunities. Secret Harbor is known for its sheltered cove and calmer water. Lindquist Beach is a good option for a quieter, more pristine beach experience. Each suits a slightly different type of visitor, so the best choice depends on your priorities.

How far is Magens Bay from the cruise port?
Travel time depends on whether your ship docks at Havensight or Crown Bay, plus traffic and route conditions. From Havensight, a taxi to Magens Bay typically takes around 20 minutes. From Crown Bay, expect slightly different timing due to the route. Magens Bay is generally considered a very manageable beach day trip for cruise passengers, with taxis available at both terminals.

Can I visit a beach and still get back to the ship on time?
Yes, as long as you plan sensibly. Use recognised transport such as the official taxi stand at your terminal, build a 30 to 45 minute buffer before your all aboard time and avoid staying at the beach until the very last minute. Organised beach excursions with experienced operators can simplify timing by planning the return journey for you.

Are beach chairs included on St Thomas beach excursions?
Some organised beach or resort style excursions include beach chairs or facilities as part of the package, while others make them available to rent at extra cost on the day. Always check the specific tour inclusions carefully before booking, the description should clearly state what is and is not included.

Is St Thomas good for a relaxing beach day?
Yes, St Thomas is one of the Caribbean's best cruise ports for a relaxing beach day. With multiple scenic beaches within 20 to 30 minutes of the cruise terminals, warm turquoise water and reliable sunshine, it is an excellent choice for passengers whose priority is simply sun, sea and sand. St Thomas Snorkeling Tour Questions

Reefs, turtles, kayaking and what to expect from a snorkeling excursion in St Thomas.

Snorkeling Tours Guide

Is St Thomas good for snorkeling?
Yes. St Thomas has clear, warm Caribbean water with reef areas, vibrant tropical fish and the chance to spot sea turtles in certain locations. Kayak and snorkel tours and sailing based excursions access protected marine environments that are difficult to reach independently, making an organised tour a good choice for snorkeling in St Thomas.

Can you snorkel with turtles in St Thomas?
Some tours visit areas where guests may have the chance to see green sea turtles in their natural habitat. It is an experience many visitors treasure. That said, wildlife sightings can never be guaranteed, the best way to describe it is as a genuine chance to see turtles, not a certainty. If spotting a sea turtle is important to you, choose a tour that specifically visits known turtle areas.

Do I need to be a strong swimmer for snorkeling tours?
You should be comfortable in open water. Some tours may offer guidance or flotation support for less confident swimmers, but snorkeling is always more enjoyable and safer when you are relaxed in the water and able to follow instructions from your guide. If you are not a confident swimmer, discuss this with the operator before booking.

Are kayak and snorkel tours difficult?
Kayak and snorkel tours are more physically active than a standard beach excursion. They suit guests who are comfortable paddling, moving in and out of the water and spending time in the sun. They are a moderate activity, not extreme, but not entirely passive either. If you prefer a more relaxed experience, a sailing based snorkel tour may suit you better than a kayak focused one.

What should I bring on a snorkeling tour?
Bring swimwear, a towel, reef safe sunscreen (oxybenzone based sunscreens are banned in the USVI), sunglasses, secure sandals or water shoes, a dry bag or waterproof phone pouch and any motion sickness tablets if you are sensitive on the water. Check your specific tour for any additional requirements or items provided. Read our full St Thomas snorkeling tours guide for more detail.

Private St Thomas Tour Questions

Flexible private excursions, custom itineraries and private transport for families and groups.

Private Tours Guide

Are private tours worth it in St Thomas?
Private tours can be very worthwhile for families, couples and small groups who want flexible timing, private transport and a more personalised route. They allow you to avoid waiting for a large group, move at your own pace and focus on the stops that matter most to you, whether that is beach time, scenic viewpoints or shopping.

Can private tours pick up from the cruise port?
Some private tours leave from the cruise ship pier, which makes the start of the day very straightforward. However, meeting point details vary by specific tour and by whether your ship docks at Havensight or Crown Bay. Always check whether pickup applies to your specific terminal before finalising your booking.

Can we choose our own itinerary on a private tour?
Some private vehicle tours are specifically designed for custom planning, allowing your group to shape the route around beaches, viewpoints, shopping or sightseeing. Others follow a more structured private route. Check the product details before booking to understand how much flexibility is built in.

Are private tours good for families?
Yes, families often prefer private tours because they allow a more relaxed pace, fewer delays and easier planning around children or mixed age groups. Private transport means you can stop when you need to, manage bags and beach kit more easily, and adjust the day without waiting for other guests.

Can private tours include beach time?
Yes. Many private St Thomas tours can include beaches such as Sapphire Beach, Magens Bay or other coastal stops, depending on the specific itinerary and your ship's departure time. Note that beach portions may not be suitable for ships departing at 2pm or earlier, so always check timing before booking.

Taxi and Transport Questions

Getting around St Thomas, taxi fares, safety and how to make sure you never miss the ship.

Are taxis available at the St Thomas cruise port?
Yes. Taxis and shared open air safari style transport are available at both Havensight and Crown Bay cruise terminals. You will usually find a designated taxi stand on exit from the terminal, with regulated fares displayed or available from the stand official. Private taxis can also be arranged for a fixed price directly with a driver.

Should I take a taxi or book a tour?
A taxi works well for a simple, self guided beach visit. A shore excursion is usually easier and more rewarding if you want multiple stops, snorkeling equipment, private transport, a local guide, or more organised cruise friendly timing. Tours also remove the uncertainty of finding return transport and managing the clock yourself.

Do taxis in St Thomas take cash or card?
Cash is widely used and always a reliable option for taxis, tips and smaller purchases in St Thomas. Some operators may accept cards, but passengers should not rely on card payments for every small transaction. St Thomas uses US dollars, so no currency exchange is needed for American travellers.

How do I avoid missing the ship?
Know your all aboard time before leaving the ship and build in a 30 to 45 minute buffer. Plan your return journey before you leave the terminal, not on the way back. Avoid last minute departures from beaches or shopping areas, and choose excursions that are sized to fit within your ship's schedule. If you are running late, call the ship's emergency number immediately.

Is transport included in all shore excursions?
Not always. Some tours include transport from the cruise pier, while others may require a taxi transfer to a marina or meeting point. Check the inclusions and meeting instructions carefully for each specific tour before booking, and factor in any additional transport time when planning your day.

Booking and Timing Questions

When to book, how long excursions take and how to plan the right day for your cruise schedule.

How early should I book St Thomas shore excursions?
Popular beach, snorkeling and private tours can sell out during peak cruise periods when multiple ships are in port on the same day. Booking in advance, ideally before your cruise begins, is sensible if there is a specific tour or experience you really want. Last minute availability is sometimes possible but never guaranteed.

How long should a St Thomas shore excursion be?
For most cruise passengers, 3 to 4.5 hours is a comfortable and realistic excursion length. This typically allows enough time to enjoy the experience properly while leaving a comfortable buffer to return to the ship before all aboard. Avoid booking excursions that leave no margin for traffic, delays or unexpected situations.

Can I do more than one activity in one day?
Yes, many cruise passengers comfortably combine beach time, sightseeing, a shopping stop or even snorkeling in a single port day. The key is not to overpack the itinerary. One great experience followed by a relaxed lunch is usually more enjoyable than three rushed stops with no time to settle.

What happens if the ship arrives late?
Policies vary by tour provider, so always review the booking terms before purchasing. Cruise friendly operators typically understand that ships run to their own schedules and may be able to accommodate some flexibility, but it is best not to assume. Confirming terms around late arrivals before you book can save confusion on the day.

Should I book through the cruise line or independently?
Cruise line tours offer the reassurance that the ship typically waits if their excursion runs late, something independent operators cannot always guarantee. However, independent shore excursions often provide better variety, smaller groups, private options and more competitive pricing. Many experienced cruisers book independently every time with no issues; the key is choosing reputable operators and checking timing carefully.

Families, Comfort and Accessibility

Family friendly excursions, accessibility considerations and what to bring for the day.

Are St Thomas shore excursions family friendly?
Yes, beach days, island sightseeing tours and private vehicle excursions are especially good for families. More active tours involving snorkeling, kayaking or hiking are better suited to older children and confident swimmers. Always check the minimum age requirements and activity level for any tour you are considering for a mixed age group.

Which St Thomas excursions are best for older passengers?
Private vehicle tours, relaxed island sightseeing routes and beach excursions with minimal walking are generally the best options for older passengers or those who prefer a comfortable, lower intensity day. Always check the stated activity level on any tour and avoid excursions that involve a lot of walking, climbing steps or physically demanding activity if that may be a concern.

Are St Thomas excursions wheelchair accessible?
Accessibility varies significantly by tour. Some private and beach tours may not be suitable for guests using wheelchairs or mobility scooters, particularly those involving uneven terrain, beach sand, boat boarding or open air vehicles. Always check the specific accessibility restrictions listed for your chosen tour, and contact the operator directly if you have specific requirements to discuss.

What should families bring for a St Thomas beach day?
For a beach day, bring swimwear for everyone, towels, reef safe sunscreen (required in the USVI), hats, sunglasses, a reusable water bottle and snacks if needed, particularly for young children. Cash or card for optional extras such as chair rentals, food and drink, or watersport activities is also useful. A dry bag to keep phones and valuables safe at the water's edge is always recommended.

Are active eco tours suitable for children?
Some kayak and snorkel eco tours may suit older children who are comfortable with physical activity and open water. However, always check the minimum age requirement, activity level and swimming requirements for the specific tour before booking. Tours designed for general families may be more appropriate than advanced active excursions for younger or less experienced children.

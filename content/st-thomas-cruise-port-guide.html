Port Orientation

Your First Look at St Thomas

St Thomas is one of the Caribbean's most popular cruise destinations, and for good reason. With two deep water cruise terminals, world class beaches, duty free shopping, and a huge range of shore excursions, a day here can be as relaxed or as action packed as you like.

This guide covers everything cruise passengers need, from the moment your ship arrives to when you're back on board. Ready to plan your perfect port day? Start with our Best St Thomas Shore Excursions guide.

Quick Answers for Cruise Passengers

🚢

Where will my ship dock?

Most large ships dock at Havensight (near Charlotte Amalie) or Crown Bay (west of downtown). Smaller ships occasionally anchor and tender passengers ashore.

⏰

How much time will I have?

Most itineraries give you 7 to 10 hours in port: usually 8am or 9am until 5pm or 6pm. Always confirm your ship's all aboard time and build in a 30-minute buffer.

💵

What currency is used?

US dollars, St Thomas is a US territory, so no currency exchange needed. Credit cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are available at both terminals.

🚕

How do I get around?

Open air safari taxis (shared vans) are the main way to get around. They're affordable and run fixed routes. Private taxis are also available and easy to find at both terminals.

📋

Do I need a passport?

As a US territory, US citizens do not need a passport to visit St Thomas, a government issued photo ID is sufficient. Non US citizens should follow their ship's guidance.

🛍️

Is the shopping really that good?

Yes, St Thomas is one of the top duty free shopping destinations in the Caribbean. Charlotte Amalie's Main Street is lined with jewellery, watches, rum, and more, often at prices below the mainland.

Port Terminals

Where Do Cruise Ships Dock?

St Thomas has two main cruise terminals, plus a tendering option for anchorage days. Here's what to expect at each.

🚢

Havensight Cruise Terminal

Most Common Terminal

The largest and most popular terminal in St Thomas, Havensight is located on the eastern edge of Charlotte Amalie harbour, making it perfectly placed for shopping, sightseeing, and excursions. The Havensight Mall sits right at the pier, with duty free shops, restaurants, and taxi stands immediately on exit.

  • ✓Walking distance to Charlotte Amalie, approx 15 to 20 mins on foot
  • ✓Duty free shopping at the Havensight Mall directly at the pier
  • ✓Taxi stand at the terminal, rates are fixed and regulated
  • ✓Easy excursion departures, most tour operators pick up here
  • ✓Can accommodate multiple large ships simultaneously
Taxi to Magens Bay Beach: Approx $7 to 9 per person (shared safari taxi) or $25 to 30 for a private taxi. Journey is around 20 minutes.
⚓

Crown Bay Cruise Center

West of Downtown

Crown Bay is located about 1.5 miles west of Charlotte Amalie and tends to receive smaller or overflow vessels. The facility has its own marina, waterfront dining options, and a small shopping area. A short taxi ride brings you into the heart of Charlotte Amalie or to the start of your excursion.

  • ✓Quieter terminal, fewer crowds than Havensight on busy days
  • ✓Crown Bay Marina with waterfront restaurants nearby
  • ✓Taxis available on exit, short 10-minute ride to downtown
  • ✓Good base for excursions heading toward Magens Bay or the north shore
  • ✓Historic Hassel Island visible across the bay
Taxi to Charlotte Amalie: Approx $5 to 7 per person (shared safari taxi), roughly 10 minutes. Excursion pickups are often arranged directly at the terminal.
🚤

Tendering Into Port

Occasionally, especially for smaller ships or on very busy days, vessels anchor in the harbour and ferry passengers ashore via tender boats. The tender dock lands passengers right in the heart of Charlotte Amalie, which is actually a very convenient arrival point for shopping and sightseeing.

Expect tender queues

Busy mornings can see waits of 20 to 40 minutes for the tender boat. Early risers get priority.

Book excursions wisely

If tendering, book shore excursions through operators who know tender timings and allow flexibility.

Return time buffer

Allow extra time to get back to the tender, lines build up in the late afternoon before departure.

Getting Around

Taxis & Transport in St Thomas

Getting around St Thomas on a cruise day is straightforward. Safari taxis, open air converted pickup trucks or vans, are the backbone of public transport and a quintessential St Thomas experience in themselves. They're affordable, shared, and run fixed routes with fixed rates.

Private taxis are also plentiful and easy to negotiate. For beach excursions and guided tours, most operators include pickup and drop off at your terminal, the most convenient option for cruise passengers.

💡
Rates are regulated

All taxi fares in the USVI are set by the government. Ask to see the rate card or check with the taxi stand official at your terminal if you're unsure.

👥
Safari taxis are shared

The standard safari taxi is shared, you may travel with other cruise passengers. It's part of the experience! For a private vehicle, ask specifically for a private taxi.

⏱️
Negotiate a return trip

When hiring a taxi to the beach, ask your driver if they'll return to pick you up at a set time. Most will for a small fee, it removes the stress of finding transport back.

🚗
Rental cars: proceed with care

Driving in St Thomas is on the left (US Virgin Islands style). Roads are narrow and hilly. For most cruise passengers, a taxi or organised excursion is far simpler and more stress free.

Expert Advice

Essential Tips for Cruise Passengers

Eight things experienced Caribbean travellers always do in St Thomas, and one very important thing they never do.

⏰

Never miss all aboard

Ships do not wait for late passengers. Know your all aboard time before leaving the dock and build in a 30-minute buffer, especially if you plan independent exploration.

🌅

Get off the ship early

Beaches and attractions are quieter before 10am. Early departures also give you more flexibility and a full day of daylight, don't linger at the ship.

📋

Book excursions in advance

The best local operators fill up fast, especially for sailing, snorkelling, and private tours. Book independently before your cruise for the widest choice and best prices.

🚕

Know the taxi rates

Fares are government regulated. Ask the taxi stand officer at the terminal for the official rate card, and confirm the total price with your driver before getting in.

☀️

Bring reef safe sunscreen

Oxybenzone and octinoxate based sunscreens are banned in the USVI to protect coral reefs. Pack reef safe alternatives before you travel, they may be pricier on the island.

💧

Stay hydrated

The Caribbean sun is fierce, especially in summer. Carry a reusable water bottle and refill it throughout the day: dehydration in the heat can quickly ruin your port day.

🛍️

Shop duty free wisely

US residents can bring back $1,600 worth of goods duty free from the USVI, significantly more than other Caribbean destinations. A good opportunity to stock up on jewellery, rum, or watches.

🐢

Respect the wildlife

If you're lucky enough to spot a sea turtle while snorkelling, observe from a distance and never touch. The islands' marine life is protected, and responsible tourism helps preserve it for everyone.

FAQ

St Thomas Cruise Port Questions

Everything first timers (and returning visitors) want to know about docking, getting around, and making the most of port day.

How do I know which terminal my cruise ship will use?
Your ship's daily programme (delivered the night before arrival) will confirm which terminal you're docking at. You can also check your cruise line's app or website in the days leading up to your visit. Most large ships use Havensight; smaller or overflow vessels tend to dock at Crown Bay. Your shore excursion operators will always confirm pickup location when you book.

Is it safe to explore St Thomas independently, without a ship's excursion?
Yes, the vast majority of cruise passengers explore St Thomas independently and have a wonderful time. The tourist areas around Havensight, Charlotte Amalie, and the main beaches are well frequented and generally safe. As with any destination, stay aware of your surroundings, use official taxis, and stick to well travelled areas. Booking local independent operators (rather than ship excursions) is perfectly standard and often gives you a better experience at a lower price.

Can I walk from the cruise terminal to Charlotte Amalie?
From Havensight, yes, it's roughly 0.5 miles along the waterfront to the edge of Charlotte Amalie's shopping district. The walk takes about 15 to 20 minutes and is mostly flat. From Crown Bay, it's 1.5 miles and involves busier roads, so most passengers take a short taxi into town. In the heat of midday, even the Havensight walk can feel demanding, a quick safari taxi ride is always an option.

What should I do if I'm worried about missing the ship?
The golden rule: know your all aboard time (printed on your key card and the daily programme) and aim to be back at the terminal 30 to 45 minutes before that time. If you're on an independent excursion and running late, call the ship's emergency number immediately. Ships do occasionally wait for verified delayed excursion groups, but they will not wait indefinitely. Planning shorter excursions, avoiding rush hour taxis, and choosing operators with a guarantee policy all help reduce this stress.

Are the beaches in St Thomas free to visit?
Most beaches are free, though Magens Bay charges a small entrance fee (around $5 per adult). Lindquist Beach (Smith Bay Park) also has a nominal entry fee for non residents. Other popular beaches like Sapphire Beach and Secret Harbor are free to access, though you'll pay for chair and umbrella rentals. All beaches in the USVI are technically public below the high tide line.

What is the weather like in St Thomas?
St Thomas enjoys a warm tropical climate year round, with temperatures typically between 75°F and 90°F (24 to 32°C). The dry season runs from December to April, the peak cruise season, with the most reliable sunshine. The wetter, slightly more humid season runs from June through November. Even in the wet season, rain often arrives as brief afternoon showers rather than all day downpours. Always pack sunscreen, a hat, and a light layer for any water excursions.

Is it worth visiting Charlotte Amalie for shopping?
Absolutely, if you're interested in duty free shopping. St Thomas is one of the Caribbean's top spots for it, Main Street is lined with jewellery stores (particularly well known for diamonds and tanzanite), watch boutiques, perfume shops, and rum distilleries. Prices are genuinely competitive versus mainland US, and the duty free allowance for returning Americans is a generous $1,600. That said, Charlotte Amalie also has real historic charm, Fort Christian, the 99 Steps, and the colourful Danish colonial architecture are all worth a look beyond the shopping.

Can I use my US mobile phone in St Thomas?
Yes, St Thomas is a US territory, so US mobile plans generally work there without roaming charges. Most major US carriers (Verizon, AT&T, T Mobile) cover the island. However, always check with your carrier before travelling, and be aware that ship Wi Fi is a separate charge. Non US travellers will want to check their roaming rates or pick up a local SIM upon arrival.

What can I do if I only have 5 to 6 hours in port?
A shorter port day is still very doable. For five to six hours, the best options are: a 3-hour snorkelling or sailing tour (back well before all aboard), a beach visit to Magens Bay or Sapphire (pick one; don't rush between multiple), or a focused shopping and sightseeing circuit around Charlotte Amalie. Avoid trying to pack in too much, one great experience beats three rushed ones.

Are ship shore excursions better than booking independently?
Ship excursions offer the peace of mind that the ship will wait for you if the tour runs late, something independent operators generally can't guarantee. They're also convenient, particularly for first time visitors. However, independent local operators typically offer smaller groups, more personalised experiences, and lower prices. The best independent operators in St Thomas are well established professionals who understand cruise schedules and factor in all aboard times when planning tours. Many experienced cruisers book independently every time without issues, the key is choosing reputable operators and leaving plenty of time to return.

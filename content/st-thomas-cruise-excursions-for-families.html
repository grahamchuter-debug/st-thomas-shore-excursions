Planning a Family Day in St Thomas from a Cruise Ship

St Thomas is one of the best Caribbean cruise ports for families because it offers beaches, sightseeing, private tours and easy island experiences within reach of the cruise terminals.

The key is choosing an excursion that matches your family's ages, energy levels and comfort in the water. Some families want a relaxed beach day, others prefer a private island tour with flexible timing, while older children may enjoy snorkeling, kayaking or eco adventure tours.

This guide highlights the best St Thomas shore excursions for families and explains which options are easiest for different ages and travel styles. You can also read our full St Thomas cruise port guide for practical arrival information.

Planning Advice

Tips for Visiting St Thomas with Kids

A little planning goes a long way on a family cruise stop. These tips will help you get the most out of your St Thomas port day without the stress.

1️⃣
Choose one main activity rather than overloading the day
Trying to fit in too much usually leads to a rushed, stressful experience for both parents and children
2️⃣
Check activity levels before booking snorkeling or kayaking tours
Always review age limits and physical requirements before booking water based or active excursions
3️⃣
Bring sunscreen, hats and water from the ship
Caribbean sun is strong, apply before you leave and reapply throughout the day, especially with children
4️⃣
Keep beach days simple for younger children
Sand, shallow water and simple logistics are usually all younger children need for a great port day
5️⃣
Use private tours if your family needs flexibility
Private transport gives you control over timing, stops and pacing, much easier than group tours with children
6️⃣
Build in return time before all aboard
Port traffic with children can be slow, always allow more time than you think you need to get back to the ship
7️⃣
Bring cash or card for snacks, drinks and beach extras
Food, beach chairs, toys and tips for guides are typically not included, small cash amounts cover most extras
8️⃣
Pack swimwear even if beach time is only optional
Children often want to swim unexpectedly, wearing swimwear from the start of the day keeps things simple
9️⃣
Avoid leaving the return journey too late
A missed ship is a serious problem, always prioritise a comfortable return buffer, especially with young children

Simpler Is Better with Kids

What to Avoid

Common Family Planning Mistakes

St Thomas is a brilliant family cruise port, but families can make the day harder by trying to do too much. These are the most common mistakes to avoid when planning a family shore excursion.

View Family Friendly Tours
⚠️
Booking an excursion that is too active for younger children
Always check activity levels and age suitability before booking snorkeling, kayaking or hiking tours
⚠️
Forgetting transport time from the cruise port
Most beaches and sightseeing spots are a drive from the terminals, factor in travel time both ways
⚠️
Trying to visit multiple beaches in one day
Pick one beach and stay, children enjoy depth of experience more than rushing between locations
⚠️
Not checking whether food, chairs or facilities are included
Confirm what is included before you arrive, beach chair rentals and food can add unexpected costs
⚠️
Leaving return transport too late
With children, returning to the port always takes longer than expected, leave early and wait on deck if needed
⚠️
Assuming all snorkeling tours suit all children
Snorkeling comfort varies widely, check minimum age, depth and swimming requirements before booking
⚠️
Forgetting reef safe sunscreen or not reapplying
Children burn quickly in the Caribbean sun, apply generously before leaving and reapply every two hours
⚠️
Not bringing cash for small extras
Snacks, tips, beach toys, locker rentals and small purchases all require cash or card, come prepared

FAQs

St Thomas Family Excursions FAQ

Common questions from families planning a St Thomas shore excursion from a cruise ship.

Is St Thomas good for families on a cruise?
Yes. St Thomas is one of the better Caribbean cruise ports for families because beaches, sightseeing and private tours are all accessible from the cruise terminals. The island has calm bays suitable for children, easy resort beaches and private tour operators who are experienced with family groups.

What is the best St Thomas excursion for families?
For many families, a beach day or private half day tour is the easiest and most enjoyable choice. Beach excursions are ideal for younger children while private tours offer the flexibility that families often need. Older children may also enjoy snorkeling or kayak adventures if they are confident in the water.

Which beach is best for families in St Thomas?
Magens Bay is the classic family friendly choice, known for its calm water and scenic surroundings. Sapphire Beach and Secret Harbor can also work well depending on your family's interests. Conditions, facilities and access can vary, always confirm details with your operator before booking.

Are snorkeling tours suitable for children?
Some snorkeling tours may suit older children and confident swimmers. Each operator sets its own age limits and swimming requirements, so always check before booking. Younger children or non swimmers may find a simple beach excursion a more comfortable choice.

Are private tours worth it for families?
Yes. Private tours can be very useful for families because they offer flexible pacing, private transport and easier planning around children. There is no large group to wait for, and you can adjust stops if children need a break or if someone needs more or less time at a particular location.

Can families do St Thomas independently from the cruise port?
Yes, especially for simple beach or shopping visits. Taxis are widely available at the cruise terminals. However, organised tours or private vehicles can make transport, timing and planning considerably easier when travelling with children, particularly for locations further from the port.

What should families avoid in St Thomas?
Avoid overloading the day with too many activities, booking excursions beyond your children's comfort level and leaving too little time to return to the ship. Trying to visit multiple beaches in one day is also a common mistake, it is usually better to choose one great beach and spend more time there.

Should families book excursions in advance?
Yes. Family friendly tours and private vehicle options can sell out on busy cruise days when multiple ships are in port. Advance booking is recommended to secure your preferred excursion and avoid disappointment at the pier.

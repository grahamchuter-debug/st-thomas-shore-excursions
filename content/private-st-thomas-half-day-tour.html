Day at a Glance

What To Expect on the Day

A Private St Thomas Half Day Tour is shaped around your group, here is how a typical day unfolds.

1

Meet at the Cruise Pier

After meeting at the agreed point near the cruise ship pier, your private vehicle and driver are ready for your group. No waiting for a full coach, just your party and the road ahead.

2

Plan Your Route with the Driver

Tell your driver your priorities early, whether that is a beach, a viewpoint, time in Charlotte Amalie or a mix of sightseeing and shopping. The driver knows the island and can help plan a realistic half day route around your ship's schedule.

3

Explore Your Chosen Stops

Depending on your preferences, possible stops may include scenic viewpoints, beaches, Charlotte Amalie, Havensight, Yacht Haven Grande, lunch stops or shopping areas. The route is yours to decide, though choosing two or three well paced stops works best in a half day.

4

Return to the Ship on Schedule

Your private driver plans the return around your ship's all aboard time. You arrive back at the pier comfortably before sail away, without the stress of shared transport running late.

Note: The exact route is best planned realistically around your ship's schedule, traffic and return time. This tour is best used to enjoy a few well chosen highlights rather than trying to see the whole island in one day.

Expert Tips

Tips for Planning Your Private St Thomas Half Day Tour

Make the most of your half day with a few practical pointers before you book and before you go.

1
Choose two or three main stops
Trying to do everything in a half day leads to a rushed tour. Two or three well chosen highlights make for a far more enjoyable experience.
2
Confirm which cruise port your ship uses
St Thomas has multiple docking areas. Make sure your operator knows your exact pier location to confirm pickup and drop off.
3
Build in return time before all aboard
Always know your ship's all aboard time and leave a comfortable buffer. Traffic near the cruise pier on busy port days can be unpredictable.
4
Check what is included before booking
Entrance fees, meals and gratuities are not usually included. Confirm exactly what your tour covers so there are no surprises on the day.
5
Bring cash or card for extras
Entrance fees, beach chairs, refreshments, shopping and tips all require payment on the day. A mix of cash and card covers most scenarios.
6
Check departure time if beach matters
If your ship departs at 2:00 p.m. or earlier, a beach stop may not fit comfortably into the tour. Plan accordingly or focus on viewpoints and sightseeing instead.
7
Tell your driver your priorities early
The more clearly you communicate what matters most to your group, the better your driver can tailor the half day route to suit everyone.
8
Keep the route realistic for a half day
St Thomas has a lot to offer, but a half day tour works best when expectations are realistic. A focused, unhurried tour of selected highlights beats an exhausting attempt to cover everything.

FAQs

Private St Thomas Half Day Tour FAQ

Common questions from cruise passengers planning a private half day island tour.

Is this tour private?
Yes. This is a private vehicle tour for your group only. You are not sharing the vehicle with other travellers.

How long is the tour?
The tour is usually around 4 hours. Exact duration may vary depending on your group's chosen stops, traffic and ship schedule. Confirm the specific timing with your operator at booking.

Can we choose our own itinerary?
Yes. This tour is designed to offer custom itinerary flexibility. You can shape the route around beaches, viewpoints, shopping, sightseeing or a mix, though the route should remain realistic for your time in port. Choosing two or three well paced stops usually works best.

Does the tour leave from the cruise port?
Yes. The tour usually leaves from the cruise ship pier. Confirm the exact meeting point with your operator before the day, as St Thomas has multiple docking areas.

Can we include a beach stop?
Yes, beach stops may be possible depending on your ship schedule and chosen route. However, beach time may not be suitable for ships departing at 2:00 p.m. or earlier. Check your all aboard time before planning a beach stop into the tour.

Are entrance fees and meals included?
Entrance fees, meals and gratuities are not usually included unless stated in the specific tour details. Bring cash or card to cover any optional extras on the day.

Do guests drive the vehicle?
No. Guests do not drive the vehicle. A professional driver handles all transport throughout the tour, allowing your group to relax and enjoy the island scenery.

Is this good for large groups?
Yes. Vehicles may accommodate groups up to around 27 guests, depending on availability and final booking details. It is one of the few private tour options suited to a larger party travelling together.

Is this suitable for families?
Yes. Families often like this tour because it offers private transport and a flexible pace. The easy activity level, custom route and private vehicle make it easier to manage children, beach stops and changing interests during the day.

Should I book in advance?
Yes. Private vehicle tours can have limited availability, especially on busy cruise days in St Thomas. Booking in advance secures your vehicle, your preferred timing and avoids disappointment on the day.
